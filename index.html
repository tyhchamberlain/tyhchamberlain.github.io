Spectra of Metric Graphs (a.k.a. Quantum Graphs)

Any metric graph is a graph \(G\) along with a metric \(\ell\) assigning lengths to its edges. The pair \((G,\ell)\) has the structure of a singular Riemannian manifold. One can then study its spectrum or the collection of eigenvalues of its Laplace operator. Eigenfunctions are subject to certain constraints at the vertices of \(G\), such as Kirkoff's current law. Researchers are often interested in the distribution of spectra and properties of eignefunctions. For instance, under what conditions is the spectrum of \((\G,\ell)\) simple, with eigenfunctions of full support?

I am interested in how the topology of \(G\) influences the behavior of the `generic' eigenfunction. The main object of focus is the secular manifold \(\Sigma_G\) which compresses the spectrum of \((\G,\ell)\) into a real analytic set. Many properties of generic eigenfunctions are encoded as analytic subsets of \(\Sigma_G\).
